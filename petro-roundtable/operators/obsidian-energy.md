## Operating Philosophy

Obsidian Energy's strategy centers on **capital discipline and per-share value growth**, not production growth for its own sake. Key tenets:

- **Cold-flow horizontal focus** — Multilateral "fishbone" wells targeting the upper Bluesky pay zone where viscosity is lowest (~80,000 cP vs 1,750,000 cP at base). No thermal recovery — the capex intensity does not justify the returns at our scale.
- **Waterflood EOR as the next lever** — Transitioning from primary cold flow to waterflood in both Bluesky (HVS) and Clearwater (Dawson, Nampa) to improve recovery factors beyond the 5-10% primary RF ceiling.
- **No thermal operations** — Unlike CNRL at Carmon Creek (CSS) or Headwater (SAGD), OBE has determined that cold-flow economics are superior for our asset base at current heavy oil differentials.
- **Per-share value growth** — Aggressive share buybacks (7.6M shares, $54.9M in 2025), debt reduction, and disciplined capital allocation. Return of capital to shareholders is a core priority alongside reinvestment.
- **Portfolio optimization** — The $325M Pembina Cardium divestiture (2024) concentrated the portfolio on Peace River heavy oil and light oil at Willesden Green/Viking, funding debt reduction and buybacks.

## Well Design & Technology

### Multilateral Fishbone — Standard Design
- **Bluesky:** 10-leg multilateral horizontal wells targeting upper Bluesky pay (upper 10-15m of the 32m gross interval)
- **Clearwater:** 8-leg multilateral horizontals
- **Rationale:** Upper pay targeting exploits the 22x viscosity gradient — best mobility oil is at the top despite best permeability at the base (see `knowledge/bluesky-formation.md`, Section 3: Vertical Viscosity Gradient)
- **Completion:** Open-hole with slotted liners, no frac — cold flow relies on wormhole propagation and foamy oil mechanisms

### Waffle Well Innovation
- OBE-proprietary well geometry being piloted at HVS
- 2 of 5 HVS wells tested at >170 boe/d, outperforming linear offset wells
- Early interventions required — technology still in pilot phase
- Potential to improve drainage pattern efficiency in Bluesky reservoir compartments

### WellFi Downhole Monitoring
- Real-time downhole pressure and temperature sensors on PCP (progressing cavity pump) equipped wells
- Dual P/T sensors enable data-driven pump optimization and early intervention
- WellFi devices tracked via device inventory system (incoming > in_stock > assigned > installed lifecycle)
- Currently deployed on Obsidian Energy's 210 active wells in the wellfi-app monitoring platform

## Competitive Position

### vs CNRL (Carmon Creek — CSS Thermal)
- CNRL operates CSS thermal at Carmon Creek targeting the same Bluesky Formation
- CNRL's approach: high capex intensity ($1B+ facility), targets deeper/thicker pay, higher RF potential (20-30% vs OBE's 5-10% primary)
- OBE's advantage: dramatically lower capex per well, faster payout, no OTSG/water treatment infrastructure
- OBE's disadvantage: lower ultimate recovery factor, limited to upper pay, constrained by viscosity gradient
- Key differentiator: OBE believes cold-flow economics are superior at current WCS differentials and company scale

### vs Baytex (Seal — Polymer Flood)
- Baytex operates polymer flood in the Seal area (same Bluesky Formation)
- Baytex's approach: 1,500 ppm polymer injection to improve sweep efficiency, rapid water breakthrough (4-6 months)
- OBE's waterflood pilot: earlier stage, starting with conventional waterflood before considering polymer
- Key comparison: Baytex's polymer has shown rapid breakthrough risk; OBE's waterflood approach is more conservative

### vs Headwater (Clearwater Focus)
- Headwater is the dominant Clearwater pure-play operator
- Headwater's advantage: Clearwater formation is shallower, lower viscosity, better primary recovery
- OBE's position: Clearwater is a secondary formation — 103 locations but Bluesky is the core asset
- Both use multilateral cold-flow horizontals; Headwater has more Clearwater operational data
