## 1. Stratigraphic Position

The Bluesky Formation sits within the **Fort St. John Group**:

```
Overlying:  Wilrich Member (Spirit River Fm) — marine shale cap rock
            ─────────────────────────────────
Target:     BLUESKY FORMATION
            ─────────────────────────────────
Underlying: Gething Formation (continental/marginal-marine)
            OR directly on Paleozoic bedrock (Debolt Fm — Mississippian)
            where Gething is absent due to pre-Cretaceous topography
```

**Correlatives:**
- Homotaxially equivalent to the "glauconitic sandstone" of the Mannville Group (central Alberta)
- Corresponds to the Wabiskaw Member at the base of the Clearwater Formation (Athabasca)

**Subsurface geometry:** Depositional wedge dipping SW. Pinches out into marine shale in extreme NW Alberta.

**Key townships:** Peace River Oil Sands (PROS) spans Townships 78-99, Ranges 7-24W5.

## 2. Depositional Environment

**Setting:** Transgressive estuarine complex deposited during the southward transgression of the **Boreal Sea** (Moosebar Sea) into the Alberta foreland basin during Aptian to early Albian.

**Key characteristics:**
- Wave-dominated estuarine complex transitioning to shoreface and offshore marine
- Top of Bluesky = marine maximum flooding surface within Fort St. John / Mannville Group
- Controlled by paleotopography of pre-Cretaceous unconformity
- Red Earth Highlands (Devonian/Mississippian carbonates) acted as physical barriers separating NE and SW accumulations
- Aggraded without significant incision — facies are uniquely preserved and mappable
- Paleotopographic lows on the eroded Mississippian unconformity acted as depocenters for thickest sands

**Trace fossils:** Dense, stacked *Rosselia* assemblages indicate storm-influenced, wave-dominated delta-front deposits — high-energy environments where clays were winnowed, leaving high-porosity sands.

## 3. Reservoir Properties by Facies

Based on core analysis from 80+ wells in Peace River area (Hubbard et al. 2002, CSPG):

| Facies | Avg Porosity | Quality | Avg Thickness | Resource Scale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tidal-Inlet** | 28.0% | Highest — massive, homogeneous sands, very little fines | ~15.8m | ~1 Billion bbl BIP locally (areally restricted) |
| **Barrier-Bar** | 26.6% | Excellent — mud-lined burrows, pebbly horizons, porosity increases upward | Bay mouth restricted | ~6.5 Billion bbl BIP (~113 sections) |
| **Flood-Tidal Delta** | 27.0% | Good — low-angle cross-bedding, IHS, bioturbation at margins | 14.4m avg, 22m max | ~176 km2 |
| **Bayhead Delta** | 26.5% | Moderate — very fine to fine-grained, upward coarsening | 12.1m avg | ~1.5 Billion bbl BIP (>38 sections) |
| **Tidal-Channel** | 24.4% | Low-to-Moderate — high variability, IHS, mud laminae restrict kv | Variable | Compartmentalized, hard to map |
| **Tidal-Flat** | <20.0% | Poor — thin (<2m), muddy, heterogeneous | <2m | Non-commercial (low transmissibility) |

**Two-unit lithological model (Carmon Creek):**

1. **Lower Sand (Deltaic/Estuarine Transition):** Medium-grained, well-sorted, chert-rich. Tidal sand bars + distributary channels. Multi-Darcy permeability. Best reservoir quality.
2. **Upper Sand (Estuarine):** Fine-grained, quartzose. Up to 18% disseminated kaolinite and illite clays. Parallel-laminated to ripple cross-laminated. Permeability drops to 100s mD. Complicates fluid injection for CEOR.

## 4. Fluid Properties

### API Gravity
- Peace River: **8-16 deg API** (heavy oil to bitumen)
- Bitumen definition (AER): density >1000 kg/m3 (<10 deg API), viscosity >10,000 cP
- Conventional pools: up to 43 deg API (Beatton River West, BC)

### Vertical Viscosity Gradient (Critical Data)
In a typical 32m thick Bluesky reservoir at Peace River:

| Position | Dead Oil Viscosity (20 degC) | Oil Mobility |
|---|---|---|
| **Top of reservoir** | ~80,000 cP | 0.017 mD/cP |
| **Base of reservoir** (near OWC) | ~1,750,000 cP | 0.000030 mD/cP |
| **Gradient** | **22x increase** over ~32m | **~500x reduction** top to base |

**Cause:** Biodegradation over geological time. Fresh, lighter oil charged the top; microbial degradation occurred at the basal oil-water contact under anaerobic conditions, concentrating heaviest oil at the bottom.

**Thermal persistence:** Viscosity heterogeneity persists even at thermal temperatures — live oil viscosity varies by up to **4x** even at 200-215 degC steam temperatures.

**Engineering implication:** Absolute permeability actually INCREASES toward the base (7x, due to cleaner chert-rich sands), but is entirely overwhelmed by the exponential viscosity increase. Result: operators must position horizontal laterals as HIGH as possible to target lower-viscosity oil, effectively stranding the oil in the highest-quality rock at the base.

### Geochemical Sources
- **Western Bluesky:** Charged from Jurassic Gordondale source rock (higher RSC/sulphur compounds, odor issues)
- **Eastern Bluesky:** Charged from Devonian/Mississippian Exshaw Formation
- Initial API gravities in the 20s before biodegradation

## 4b. Structural Framework

**Regional structure:** The Bluesky Formation sits within the Peace River region, influenced by the **Peace River Arch** — a major basement-rooted structural high that controlled sedimentation patterns throughout the Phanerozoic. The arch influenced Bluesky deposition by controlling accommodation space and sediment routing.

**Stress regime:** Normal faulting stress regime (Sv > SHmax > Shmin), typical of the Western Canadian Sedimentary Basin at these depths. This controls:
- Fracture orientation (NE-SW dominant in Peace River area)
- Wellbore stability during horizontal drilling
- Maximum injection pressures for CSS (must stay below fracture gradient to avoid cap rock breach)

**Faulting:** Minor faulting is present but not extensively mapped in most Bluesky development areas. Faults can act as:
- Fluid conduits (risk: water influx pathways from basal aquifer)
- Compartment boundaries (risk: isolated drainage areas reducing well recovery)

**Data gap:** Detailed structural mapping from 3D seismic is available for Carmon Creek and some Seal area developments, but much of the Peace River Bluesky lacks 3D seismic coverage. Well-to-well correlation and 2D seismic are the primary structural tools in many areas.

## 4c. Geomechanical Properties

The Bluesky sandstone is **semi-consolidated to unconsolidated**, which drives both its excellent porosity and its sand production challenges.

**Key geomechanical parameters (typical ranges for Peace River Bluesky):**

| Parameter | Typical Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| UCS (Unconfined Compressive Strength) | <1-5 MPa | Very weak rock; lower values in clean sands |
| Young's Modulus | 0.5-3 GPa | Low stiffness, highly deformable |
| Poisson's Ratio | 0.25-0.35 | Varies with clay content and saturation |
| Friction Angle | 25-35 deg | Controls shear failure during drawdown |
| Cohesion | 0.1-1.0 MPa | Minimal cementation in most facies |

**Engineering implications:**
- **Sand production:** Low UCS + high drawdown = sand failure. CHOPS exploits this deliberately; thermal/CEOR wells must prevent it with slotted liners.
- **Thermal cycling:** CSS expansion/contraction cycles progressively weaken near-wellbore rock. Liner design must account for multiple cycles.
- **Wellbore stability:** Low cohesion means horizontal laterals in fishbone wells can be prone to collapse, especially in clay-rich upper sand. Drilling fluid weight and open-hole completion timing are critical.
- **Cap rock integrity:** Wilrich shale geomechanical properties must be evaluated per AER Directive 086 for any thermal project. Maximum operating pressure (MOP) is constrained by fracture gradient through the cap rock.

**Data gap:** Geomechanical properties vary significantly with facies, depth, clay content, and saturation state. Site-specific testing (triaxial compression, acoustic log calibration) is essential for each development area.

## 5. Trapping Mechanism

**Primary seal:** Wilrich Member of Spirit River Formation — widespread, thick marine shale deposited during major regional transgression. Excellent continuous cap rock preventing upward hydrocarbon migration. Wilrich thickness is typically 20-50m in the Peace River area, providing robust containment for thermal operations.

**Lateral trapping:** Estuarine sand body pinchout against the Red Earth Highlands (exposed carbonate paleohighlands). Oil migrated updip from the deep basin and accumulated beneath Wilrich shales and against carbonate barriers.

**Bottom water / Basal aquifer:** Formation frequently lacks a definitive impermeable shale barrier separating oil column from basal water aquifers. This is a major production challenge (see Section 8).

**Aquifer characterization:**
- The basal aquifer is connected to the regional Mannville/Bluesky aquifer system
- Aquifer strength varies: some areas show strong aquifer support (rapid water coning), others show limited aquifer (pressure depletion dominates)
- Salinity: Bluesky formation waters are typically brackish to saline (TDS 10,000-50,000+ mg/L), varying by area
- Aquifer pressure: Near-hydrostatic to slightly overpressured in some thermal areas
- **Data gap:** Aquifer extent, transmissibility, and connectivity are poorly characterized in many areas — a major uncertainty for water management planning and thermal project design

## 6. Production Methods

### Primary Cold Flow

**CHOPS (Cold Heavy Oil Production with Sand):**
- Deliberately produces unconsolidated formation sand alongside heavy oil
- Sand removal creates branching "wormholes" extending deep into reservoir
- Pressure drawdown induces **foamy oil flow** (dissolved gas exsolves, bubbles expand, drive oil)
- Recovery factor: **5-15%** (up to 90% of oil left behind)
- Low capex but fundamentally inefficient

**Multilateral Horizontal Drilling ("Fishbone"):**
- Pioneered by Baytex and Obsidian Energy in Peace River Bluesky
- Single motherbore with multiple open-hole laterals branching horizontally
- Laterals steered with precision to stay in **upper, lower-viscosity strata** (oil mobility 500x higher at top)
- Reduces pressure drawdown needed, enables economic cold production
- Delays onset of basal water coning
- Key to current Obsidian Energy production strategy

### Thermal EOR

**Cyclic Steam Stimulation (CSS):**
- Most effective primary thermal method in Peace River (Shell/CNRL at Carmon Creek)
- Three stages: injection → soak → production
- Steam injection: ~1,000 tons/day per well for weeks/months
- Horizontal wells to 1,400m total length with 500m horizontal sections
- **Critical constraint:** Sw must be <31% (deep resistivity <40 ohm.m) — higher Sw = mobile water acts as thermal heat sink

**SAGD (limited in Bluesky):**
- Standard SAGD challenging in Bluesky due to thinner pay, shale baffles limiting vertical steam rise
- **Fast-SAGD** proposed: Standard dual-well pairs + offset CSS wells ~50m away to accelerate lateral steam chamber growth

**In-Situ Combustion (THAI):**
- Toe-To-Heel Air Injection piloted at 550m depth
- Generates heat within reservoir without surface water consumption
- Lab tests show difficulty sustaining high-temp combustion due to low coke deposition rate

### Chemical EOR (CEOR)

**Polymer Flooding:**
- Murphy Oil pilot at Seal area: dead oil viscosity 5,000-12,000 cP
- Horizontal injectors, polymer at ~1,500 ppm (20-30 cP viscosity)
- Result: production increase sustained for months, but rapid water breakthrough (4-6 months)

**ASP Flooding (Mooney Field — landmark case):**
- 16 deg API oil, in-situ viscosity 150-1,500 cP
- Primary recovery: 4-5%; waterflood added only 3%
- ASP cocktail: alkali (NaOH/Na2CO3) + surfactant (ultra-low IFT) + polymer (mobility control)
- **Result:** Production from 175 BOPD (2011) to 2,600 BOPD (2013) — transformative
- STOOIP: 141 million bbl; targeting ~50 million bbl with CEOR
- Confirms CEOR viable in viscous, low-temperature Bluesky sandstone

## 8. Challenges

### Water Coning / Bottom Water
- No definitive impermeable shale barrier between oil column and basal aquifer
- Extreme mobility ratio between injected fluids and native heavy oil
- Water breakthrough in 4-6 months in closely spaced polymer flood wells
- Steam injection where Sw >31% → water acts as heat sink, destroys thermal efficiency
- **Mitigation:** Position laterals high in pay, limit drawdown, monitor Sw carefully

### Sand Production / Geomechanical Instability
- Semi-consolidated to unconsolidated sandstone
- CHOPS deliberately uses sand failure (wormholes), but erosion of downhole/surface equipment
- Thermal/CEOR wells need sand exclusion — slotted/pre-perforated liners
- Poisson's ratio and Young's modulus vary with depth, clay content, lithology
- Multiple thermal expansion/contraction cycles (CSS) compound integrity risk

### Foamy Oil Complexity
- Dissolved gas exsolves below bubble point, forms metastable foam
- Enhances CHOPS production but is poorly characterized and hard to model
- Foam behavior is pressure-, temperature-, and composition-dependent

### Shale Baffles
- IHS (inclined heterolithic stratification) in tidal channels and flood-tidal deltas
- Mud laminae restrict vertical permeability (kv) significantly
- Limits SAGD steam chamber vertical growth
- Makes CSS more favorable than SAGD in many Bluesky areas

### Emulsification (CEOR)
- Surfactant/alkali interaction with complex Bluesky crude generates stable O/W emulsions
- Can blind separation facilities and overwhelm water treatment plants
- Chemical concentrations must be rigorously minimized to control downstream costs

### Sulphur Compounds
- Gordondale-sourced oil (western Bluesky) generates higher RSC (Reduced Sulphur Compounds)
- Odor and environmental emissions issues during surface handling
- AER has specific Peace River odor/emissions regulations

## 9. Key Data Points for Calculations

### Volumetric OOIP Formula
```
OOIP (bbl) = 7758 * A * h * phi * So / Bo

Where:
  A   = area (acres)
  h   = net pay thickness (ft)
  phi = porosity (fraction)
  So  = oil saturation (fraction) = 1 - Sw
  Bo  = formation volume factor (reservoir bbl / STB)
  7758 = bbl per acre-ft
```

### Recovery Factors by Method
| Method | Typical RF | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| CHOPS | 5-15% | Wormhole-dependent, foamy oil |
| Multilateral cold flow | 10-20% | Fishbone architecture, upper pay targeting |
| CSS | 20-30% | Bluesky dominant thermal method |
| SAGD | 40-60% | Limited in Bluesky due to shale baffles |
| Polymer flood | 8-15% incremental | Rapid water breakthrough limits |
| ASP flood | 15-25% incremental | Mooney field: 4% primary → 25%+ with ASP |
| Primary waterflood | 3-5% incremental | Poor mobility ratio |

### Key Petrophysical Cutoffs (Thermal Net Pay — Carmon Creek)
| Parameter | Cutoff | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Thickness | >=10m continuous | Minimum for thermal well placement |
| Clay Volume (Vcl) | <40% | From normalized GR |
| Water Saturation (Sw) | <31% | Above 31% = mobile water = heat sink |
| Top Gas | Exclude entirely | N-D crossover = thief zones |
