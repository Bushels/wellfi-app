## 6. Well Design & Completions

### Multilateral Horizontal ("Fishbone") Architecture

OBE's primary well design is the cold-flow multilateral horizontal — a single motherbore with multiple open-hole laterals branching horizontally. No slotted liners are needed for primary production. Pad drilling with 2-4 wells per pad is typical.

| Formation | Well Design | DCET Capex | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bluesky | 10-leg multilateral, open hole | $3.3M | Corporate Presentation Slide 27 |
| Clearwater | 8-leg multilateral, open hole | $2.3M | Corporate Presentation Slide 27 |
| Cardium (WG) | 2.0 mile horizontal | $4.5M | Corporate Presentation Slide 27 |
| Belly River (WG) | 1.6 mile horizontal | $5.5M | Corporate Presentation Slide 27 |
| West Viking | 1.8 mile horizontal | $2.1M | Corporate Presentation Slide 27 |

**Lateral steering rationale:** Laterals are steered with precision to stay in the upper Bluesky pay where oil mobility is 500x higher than at the base of the reservoir. This is driven by the 22x vertical viscosity gradient documented in the Bluesky Formation — see `knowledge/bluesky-formation.md` Section 4 (Fluid Properties — Vertical Viscosity Gradient). Dead oil viscosity at the top of pay is ~80,000 cP vs ~1,750,000 cP at the base, creating an oil mobility of 0.017 mD/cP at the top vs 0.000030 mD/cP at the base. | bluesky-formation.md Section 4

### Waffle Well Innovation

OBE tested a "waffle well" design on 2 of 5 HVS wells in 2025. Both produced >170 boe/d (100% oil). The waffle design showed better performance than standard linear offset wells, though 2 required early interventions. The design increases reservoir contact area on existing pads. Ongoing evaluation. | Q4/FY2025 Results press release; 2026 Guidance press release

## 8. Why No Thermal

OBE operates exclusively cold-flow in the Bluesky — no CSS, no SAGD. This is a deliberate strategic choice, not a limitation:

**1. Viscosity is manageable at top of pay.** Bluesky dead oil viscosity at Peace River (~550m depth, ~25 degC reservoir temperature) is ~80,000 cP at the top of pay — low enough for primary production with multilateral horizontals. OBE's 10-leg fishbone wells target this upper zone where oil mobility is 0.017 mD/cP. Cross-reference: `knowledge/bluesky-formation.md` Section 4 (Vertical Viscosity Gradient — 22x increase over ~32m, ~500x mobility reduction top to base).

**2. Capital intensity advantage.** Thermal recovery (CSS/SAGD) requires once-through steam generators (OTSGs), water treatment facilities, and steam distribution infrastructure. Capital intensity of thermal is 3-5x higher per flowing barrel compared to cold-flow multilateral drilling. | bluesky-formation.md Section 6, Section 9 (Break-Even Prices)

**3. Break-even economics favor cold-flow.** OBE's cold-flow break-even is WCS $35-50/bbl vs CSS break-even of WCS $45-60/bbl. | bluesky-formation.md Section 9 (Break-Even Prices)

**4. Waterflood is the chosen EOR path.** Rather than thermal escalation, OBE is pursuing waterflood EOR — lower capex, leverages existing infrastructure, and is expected to reduce decline rates over time. $22M allocated in 2026 for waterflood expansion. | 2026 Capital Program

**5. CNRL uses CSS at Carmon Creek for different geological reasons.** CNRL's Carmon Creek targets deeper, thicker pay in the deltaic lower sand of the Bluesky where viscosity is higher (approaching 1,750,000 cP at the base). CNRL's CSS horizontal wells reach 1,400m total depth with 500m horizontal sections. Cross-reference: `knowledge/bluesky-formation.md` Section 3 (Reservoir Properties by Facies — two-unit lithological model), Section 6 (Production Methods — CSS). | bluesky-formation.md Sections 3, 4, 6

**6. Formation facies targeting.** OBE targets the barrier-bar and flood-tidal delta facies of the upper Bluesky — high porosity (26-28%), excellent reservoir quality, and lower viscosity. CNRL targets both the upper estuarine and lower deltaic sands where the highest-quality rock (multi-Darcy permeability) unfortunately contains the highest-viscosity oil. Cross-reference: `knowledge/bluesky-formation.md` Section 3 (Reservoir Properties by Facies). | bluesky-formation.md Section 3

## 10. Technology & Monitoring (WellFi)

OBE is deploying WellFi as the first downhole monitoring tool in the Bluesky Formation. WellFi provides dual pressure/temperature sensors delivering real-time bottomhole pressure (BHP) data.

**Operational applications:**
- **Pump optimization:** Real-time BHP enables PCP (Progressive Cavity Pump) speed tuning without pulling well
- **Intervention timing:** Data-driven decisions replace scheduled workovers — intervene when sensors show degradation, not on calendar
- **Production decline diagnosis:** Distinguish between pump failure, sand loading, and reservoir pressure depletion using pressure trends
- **Waterflood response monitoring:** Track pressure communication between injectors and producers in real time

**Reference data:** Well-level WellFi data available for analysis at `Data/Obsidian/Wells/102161808317W509/` (Well 102-16-18-083-17W5) including production CSV, directional survey, PCP chart, WellFi sensor data (BHP, dual sensors), and pressure analysis. Additional sensor data at `Data/Obsidian/Wells/102130708317W51308/` (Well 102-13-07-083-17W5). | Data files

**Directional survey reference:** `Data/Obsidian/Wells/OBE Hz Walrus (8-28) 1-29-82-18W5 Final Survey L01.csv` — Walrus field directional survey for trajectory analysis. | Data files
